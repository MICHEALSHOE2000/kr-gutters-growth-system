# K&R Gutters Inc — Client Acquisition System

Live demo: https://kr-gutters-growth.aderintomicheal6.chatgpt.site

This repository is a conversion-focused redesign and growth proposal for **K&R Gutters Inc**, serving Metro Atlanta. It is designed to turn local search traffic into qualified calls and quote requests—not merely to look newer.

## What the website fixes

- One dominant promise: prevent expensive water damage.
- Phone and free-quote actions visible immediately on desktop and mobile.
- Separate, clear paths for cleaning, repair, installation and gutter guards.
- Existing trust assets surfaced early: insured, family-owned, warranty and customer reviews.
- Before-and-after work positioned as proof.
- Short, trackable quote form with service and ZIP-code qualification.
- Metro Atlanta service-area content and search-friendly page structure.

## 14-day pilot plan

### Days 1–2 — Tracking and baseline

- Install GA4 and Google Tag Manager.
- Track phone clicks, quote submissions and booked estimates as conversions.
- Connect Google Ads call reporting where available.
- Record current Google Business Profile visibility, site traffic and lead volume.
- Define a qualified lead: in service area, needs an offered gutter service and provides working contact details.

### Days 3–5 — Search campaign launch

- Create tightly grouped Google Search campaigns for gutter cleaning, repair, seamless installation and gutter guards.
- Target only approved Metro Atlanta service areas.
- Use high-intent keywords such as “gutter cleaning near me,” “gutter repair Atlanta” and “gutter guard installation.”
- Add negative keywords to remove jobs, DIY, supplies, wholesale and unrelated searches.
- Send each ad group to the most relevant page/section, not a generic homepage.
- Use call, location, sitelink and structured-snippet assets.

### Days 6–10 — Conversion and follow-up

- Review search terms daily and block wasted traffic.
- Test the water-damage angle against the 30-minute quote promise.
- Route every form lead into a simple CRM pipeline: New → Contacted → Estimate Scheduled → Won/Lost.
- Trigger an immediate confirmation email or SMS after a quote request.
- Create follow-ups for 5 minutes, 24 hours and 3 days when a lead has not booked.

### Days 11–14 — Optimize and report

- Shift budget toward services and ZIP codes generating qualified leads.
- Compare calls versus forms, mobile versus desktop and each service line.
- Report spend, qualified leads, cost per qualified lead, scheduled estimates and closed jobs.
- Recommend the next 30-day budget using real pilot data.

## Local SEO and GEO plan

1. Fully optimize the Google Business Profile categories, services, photos, service areas, products and Q&A.
2. Publish distinct pages for gutter cleaning, repairs, seamless installation and gutter guards.
3. Build truthful location pages for priority cities where K&R actually works.
4. Add LocalBusiness, Service, FAQ and review schema using verified business information.
5. Standardize the company name, phone and address/service-area details across major directories.
6. Request reviews after completed jobs with a direct link and prompts that naturally mention the service and city.
7. Publish useful homeowner content around Georgia rainfall, seasonal cleaning and warning signs.
8. Structure concise, factual answers so search engines and AI assistants can understand and cite K&R's services.

## Lead follow-up workflow

```text
Ad / Local Search
        ↓
Service landing page
        ↓
Tracked call or quote form
        ↓
Instant confirmation + owner notification
        ↓
CRM: New lead
        ↓
Call within 5 minutes
        ↓
Estimate scheduled → reminder → completed job → review request
```

## Success measurement

The pilot does **not** count clicks as success. The scorecard is:

- Qualified calls and quote requests
- Cost per qualified lead
- Estimate-booking rate
- Lead response time
- Estimates issued
- Jobs won and estimated revenue
- Revenue-to-ad-spend ratio once job values are available

Google Ads spend is paid directly by K&R through its own account. All accounts, campaign data and tracking remain under K&R's ownership.

## Run locally

```bash
npm install
npm run dev
```
